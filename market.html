The Problem

  • Government issued land records is one of the key document used in lending.

  • Fraud can be complicated to understand, and often misused by stakeholders, despite digitisation.

  • Quite difficult to keep track of millions of such documents manually, especially constant changes in them.

  • Banks on an average are permitted to revisit this document, once every 3 years, giving very large window for possible frauds.

  • Estimated that $700 million is being paid in bribes at land registrars across India

    Our Agri Intelligence Engine utilises remote sensing data alongside a range of traditional and alternative data points to assess a farmer’s creditworthiness.


    Continuous monitoring of farmlands, capturing changes in vegetation/crop cover and providing an early warning system for repayment risk.
